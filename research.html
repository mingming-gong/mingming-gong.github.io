Research (Selected Topics)

Causal Discovery

Causal discovery is the problem of inferring the causal relationships from observational data. It is a fundamental task in various disciplines of science and engineering. We are working on computational methods to discover causal relationships from real-world complex data, with theoretical guarantees.

  • Causal discovery from imperfectly sampled time series data [ICML'15][ICML'15][UAI'17][NeurIPS'19]

  • Causal discovery from nonstationary data [ICML'19]

  • Causal discovery from data with measurement error / missing values [UAI'18][AAAI'20]


Transfer Learning

Transfer learning is a research problem that focuses on utilizing previously gained knowledge to solve new problems. For example, when trying to recognize an object at night, we can transfer the knowledge gained when learning to recognize the object during the day. We are working on exploiting causal generative process of the data to develop novel machine learning models that can adapt/generalize to new data distributions. In the context of image data, we are interested in unsupervised learning models to translate images into different styles.

  • Domain adaptation/generalization from a causal perspective [AAAI'15][ICML'16][AAAI'18][ECCV'18] [AISTATS'19][ICML'20][ICML'20] [NeurIPS'20][NeurIPS'20][NeurIPS'21]

  • Generalization in reinforcement learning [ICLR'22]

  • Unsupervised image-to-image translation [CVPR'19][CVPR'19][ICCV'21]


3D Computer Vision

3D computer vision aims to model and understand the visual world by using 3D visual information. We are interested in deep learning methods for depth estimation, 3D reconstruction, 3D model retrieval, etc. Because the current deep learning-based methods can hardly generalize to new environment, one of our research goal is to develop new deep models that can generalize across different scenarios.

  • Depth estimation [CVPR'18][CVPR'19]

  • 3D model retrieval [NeurIPS'20]

  • Interest points [IJCV'22]

  • Benchmark dataset [IJCV'21]
